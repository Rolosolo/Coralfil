## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

> *© 2026 Coralfil Inc. — CONFIDENTIAL*

### 1.1 Mission

Coralfil develops molecularly intelligent restoration substrates that ionically attract and chemically signal coral settlement. Our flagship product **Coralstick**—powered by **Reefmaker AI**—uses chitosan-alginate ionic attraction and species-specific bioactive compounds to achieve 76.8% first-attempt synthesis success and 80%+ coral attachment rates.

### 1.2 The Problem

| Issue | Impact | Current Solutions |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Generic substrates fail to attract coral | 40-60% mortality | Passive concrete, ceramic |
| No ionic/molecular adhesion mechanism | Pellets dislodge in surge | Mechanical attachment only |
| Lack of species-specific signaling | Wrong coral recruits, slow growth | Hope-based natural recovery |
| Biofilm competition | Algae overgrowth kills settlers | Manual cleaning, toxic antifoulants |

### 1.3 The Solution: Coralstick + Reefmaker AI

**Coralstick Pellets:**
- **Ionic Attraction:** Chitosan (polycation) + Alginate (polyanion) create electrostatic bond with coral polyp mucus
- **Molecular Design:** AI-optimized biopolymer matrix for site-specific degradation (60-180 days)
- **Bioactive Payload:** Nutrients, probiotics, pH buffers tailored to local stressors
- **Red Algae Biofilm:** Proprietary "scent" technology attracts specific coral spores

**Reefmaker AI Platform:**
- **NVIDIA BioNeMo:** MegaMolBART designs chitosan-alginate crosslinking ratios; ReaSyn v2 validates synthesis
- **TAO Toolkit:** Field photo analysis → species ID → formulation matching
- **Knowledge Graph:** Links site conditions → ionic requirements → settlement success

## 2. PRODUCT PORTFOLIO & ROADMAP

> *© 2026 Coralfil Inc. — CONFIDENTIAL*

### 2.1 Coralstick (Current: Phase 2 Development)

| Component | Function | AI Optimization |
|---|---|---|
| **Chitosan** (85% deacetylated) | Polycationic attraction to negatively charged coral mucus | Degree of deacetylation tuned to local pH |
| **Alginate** (high-G, high-M blends) | Polyanionic crosslinking, gel formation | G/M ratio matched to temperature/salinity |
| **Genipin/EDC crosslinker** | Covalent stabilization, tunable degradation | Crosslink density → degradation rate (60-180 days) |
| **Aragonite microspheres** | Calcium source, pH buffering, structural integrity | Size distribution for porosity control |
| **Probiotic payload** (optional) | Symbiodiniaceae, nitrogen-fixing bacteria | Species-specific microbiome matching |

**Formulation Variants (Reefmaker AI-Generated):**

| Variant | Environment | Key Additives | Degradation | Target Coral |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Coralstick-T** (Thermal) | High SST, bleaching risk | Zinc, manganese, antioxidant peptides | 90 days | Thermally resilient Acropora |
| **Coralstick-S** (Sediment) | High turbidity, siltation | Anti-siltation surface, fast-setting adhesive | 60 days | Sediment-tolerant Porites |
| **Coralstick-A** (Algal competition) | High algae pressure | Natural furanones, elevated pH buffer | 120 days | Competitive Montipora |
| **Coralstick-N** (Nutrient-poor) | Oligotrophic waters | Slow-release N/P, iron | 180 days | Nutrient-efficient Pocillopora |

**Specifications:**
- Size: 3-5mm diameter pellets
- Density: 1.05-1.15 g/cm³
- Compression strength: >15 MPa
- Adhesion strength: >2 MPa shear
- Biocompatibility: ISO 10993-5 compliant
